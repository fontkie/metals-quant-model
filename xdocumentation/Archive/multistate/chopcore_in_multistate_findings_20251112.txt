QUESTION 1: WHY TRENDIMPULSE GETS DESTROYED IN MEDIUM/HIGH VOL
================================================================================

SHORT ANSWER
------------
TrendImpulse is a speedboat in stormy seas. Its shorter holding period creates 
FIVE compounding problems in elevated volatility that don't affect TrendMedium:

1. False breakout frequency (noise triggers signals)
2. Transaction cost amplification (more turnover, wider spreads)
3. Momentum decay acceleration (by the time TI enters, move is exhausted)
4. Signal instability (single outlier days flip positions)
5. Volatility regime mismatch (smaller positions + more turnover = death)

THE SMOKING GUN
---------------
Win rate barely changes across vol regimes (40-42%), but returns collapse:

                LOW VOL     MEDIUM VOL    HIGH VOL
TrendImpulse:   +10.3%      -6.4%         -5.5%
Win Rate:       41.6%       40.9%         41.6%

This is textbook whipsaw: winning and losing with equal frequency, but wins 
are tiny (exits too early) and losses are large (enters after extension).

CONTRAST: TRENDMEDIUM STABILITY
--------------------------------
TrendMedium's longer timeframe filters noise:

                LOW VOL     MEDIUM VOL    HIGH VOL
TrendMedium:    +2.2%       +2.6%         +1.8%
Sharpe:         0.589       0.612         0.402

TM actually IMPROVES in medium vol (0.612 Sharpe) because it captures genuine 
trend changes while ignoring intraday volatility spikes.

THE SPEEDBOAT VS CARGO SHIP ANALOGY
------------------------------------
Low vol (calm seas):
  ‚Ä¢ TI (speedboat): Zips around efficiently, catches quick moves
  ‚Ä¢ TM (cargo ship): Plods along steadily, slower but consistent
  ‚Üí Both profitable, TI faster

High vol (stormy seas):
  ‚Ä¢ TI (speedboat): Tossed around, constant course corrections, wasting fuel
  ‚Ä¢ TM (cargo ship): Maintains heading, ignores small waves
  ‚Üí TM makes better progress despite being "slower"

IMPLICATION: Don't fix TI - ROTATE IT
--------------------------------------
TI is working as designed. The problem is using it in wrong conditions.

Solution: Regime-aware allocation
  ‚Ä¢ LOW vol: Give TI 35% weight (it shines)
  ‚Ä¢ MEDIUM vol: Give TI 0% weight (eliminate bleeding)
  ‚Ä¢ HIGH vol: Give TI 0% weight (disaster zone)

You're not modifying the strategy, you're being selective about deployment.
